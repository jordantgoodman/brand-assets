POPS — Lowercase "l" Audit

Every font at 96px bold showing l I 1 — plus key POPS words at body size. Which "l" reads as institutional authority vs. soft/feminine?

The question: Nearly every modern sans-serif adds a tail or hook to the lowercase "l" for disambiguation from "I" and "1." The tail is a legibility feature — but for POPS, it can read as soft. This page shows the actual glyph so you can judge which fonts cross the line and which are acceptable.

How to read this: Look at the large "l I 1" glyphs. If the "l" has any curve, tail, or hook at the bottom, it'll be visible at 96px. Then check the body text — does the curve register at 13px, or does it disappear? A font might have a technically curved "l" that reads as straight at body size. The POPS brand needs one that reads as straight at both scales.
